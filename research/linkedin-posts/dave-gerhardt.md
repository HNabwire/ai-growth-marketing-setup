I think we're building the best community for B2B VPs / CMOs.
This is something we launched last year and now have 60+ members in.
Here's why it's working:
CMOs always tell us it’s lonely at the top.
You’d think that when you climb the ranks, you have all the support needed to do your job successfully, but it’s actually the opposite that happens. 
No one on your team understands your world because you are dealing with more strategy vs execution. 
And the other executives in the company aren’t marketers. So sure, you can speak about management things, but not marketing leadership in the way you want.
So this leave you feeling lonely. It can make you unsure of your decisions. And also feeling blind to what else is going on out there. You feel out of the loop.
Why is why peer communities like the Exit Five CMO Council exist. 
It’s the only space online (and in-person) where you can meet new CMO friends, talk shop, and just vent about career and company-related stuff. 
We have 60+ members today: all VPs and CMOs, leading the department, doing >$50M in revenue per year.

How Ramp is building an AI-pilled marketing team
A few months ago I met Drew Pinta (he supports marketing in a growth/data science at Ramp) on one of our webinars and thought he came across as an impressive guy. Useful and specific guidance, how to think about marketing strategy, and some key lessons they've learned at Ramp.
So I had him on for a 1:1 podcast and we talked about it:
- What it actually looks like to measure marketing when the hardest things to measure are often the ones working hardest (!)
- Drew breaks down how Ramp uses incrementality testing and event studies to put real numbers on brand stunts, how they split budget 70/30 between proven channels and experimental bets, and how scanning thousands of Gong calls with AI surfaced attribution insights that contradicted every model they had. 
- He also makes the bull case for why marketers might be better positioned than engineers in the age of AI (and doesn't shy away from the bear case either) but we also talked about the push inside of Ramp for AI adoption across the team.
If you like my podcast and think it's useful for B2B marketers you will enjoying hearing from Drew.
Love this soundbite here: "MEASUREMENT SHOULD MEET THE MARKETING WHERE IT IS" nice.
On my YouTube channel now and everywhere podcasts are these days.

### Insight:
- Focuses on modern B2B marketing measurement and attribution challenges
- Introduces advanced concepts like incrementality testing and AI-driven insights
- Shows practical application of AI in marketing teams
